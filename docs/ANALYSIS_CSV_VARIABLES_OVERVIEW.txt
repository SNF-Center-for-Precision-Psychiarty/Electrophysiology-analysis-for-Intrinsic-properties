Analysis CSV per-sweep variables overview
Per-sweep numeric variables: 43 fixed metrics plus 2×n_bins dynamic bin columns (bin_*_spike_count and bin_*_cv_isi).

GROUP 1:
Resting membrane potential
run_analysis.py (line 1247)
How it’s computed: mean of the mV signal within each sweep’s baseline window (baseline_start_s → baseline_end_s from sweep_config.json).
Assumption: the baseline window is truly pre‑stimulus and stable, so its mean reflects the resting membrane potential for that sweep.

GROUP 2:
Spike detection (per‑peak → per‑sweep averages)
spike_detection_new.py (per‑peak calculations ~lines 520–740; per‑sweep averages ~lines 1141–1156)

High‑level flow (plain English):
1) For each sweep, the code detects peaks (spikes) in the stimulus window.
2) For each detected peak, it builds two small windows:
	 • Window 1 (upstroke): a few ms before the peak up to the peak.
	 • Window 2 (downstroke): from the peak to the next peak (or ~20 ms after).
3) It computes dV/dt (slope) inside each window using np.gradient.
4) It derives per‑peak values (threshold, peak, upstroke, etc.).
5) It averages those per‑peak values across all valid peaks in the sweep.

Key per‑peak points (intuition + how they’re computed):
- Peak voltage (v_peak): the voltage at the detected peak index.
- Upstroke point (t_upstroke, v_upstroke): the time/voltage where dV/dt is maximal in Window 1 (steepest rise).
- Threshold point (t_threshold, v_threshold): compute a dV/dt threshold = threshold_percent × max_dvdt,
	then take the first index in Window 1 where dV/dt crosses that value. This marks the start of the fast rise.

Index/time conversion note:
- The “max dV/dt” index and “threshold” index are found inside Window 1 (relative to that small slice).
- We convert back to the full sweep by adding w1_start_idx, then use time[abs_idx] and voltage[abs_idx].
	That gives the true sweep‑level time/voltage.

Listed metrics (per‑sweep averages):
- avg_peak_voltage_mV: average of each peak’s v_peak.
- avg_threshold_voltage_mV: average of each peak’s v_threshold.
- avg_threshold_to_peak_mV: average of (v_peak − v_threshold).
- avg_threshold_to_peak_ms: average of (t_peak − t_threshold) in ms.
- avg_upstroke_mVms: average of max dV/dt in Window 1 (steepest rise, mV/ms).
- avg_upstroke_to_peak_ms: average of (t_peak − t_upstroke) in ms.
- avg_upstroke_to_peak_mV: average of (v_peak − v_upstroke).
- avg_downstroke_mVms: average of min dV/dt in Window 2 (steepest rise, mV/ms)
- avg_peak_to_downstroke_ms: average of (t_peak − t_downstroke)
- avg_peak_to_trough_ms: average of (t_peak − t_fast_trough)
- avg_peak_to_trough_mV: average of (v_peak − v_fast_trough)
- avg_fast_trough_at_hyperpolarization_mV: average of fast trough value (mV)
- avg_threshold_fast_trough_width_ms: average of (t_fast_trough - t_threshold)
- avg_height_mV: average of (v_peak - v_fast_trough)
- avg_half_height_mV: average of voltage value halfway between peak and fast trough (v_fast_trough + 0.5*height_mV)
- avg_ap_width_ms: average of (t_half_height_down - t_half_height_up)
- avg_upstroke_downstroke_ratio: average of (max dvdt / abs(min dvdt))
- spike_frequency_Hz: averaged firing rate over the analysis window (len(peak_voltages)/(t_max-t_min))
- mean_isi_ms: Average time between spikes 
- cv_isi: How regular the firing is (std(isi)/mean(isi)) (0 = very regular)
- middle_first_AP_width_ratio: average of ratio of ap_width between middle and first AP
- middle_first_AP_peak_threshold_ratio: average of ratio of (v_peak - v_threshold) between middle and first AP
- middle_first_AP_fast_trough_ratio: average of ratio of v_fast_trough between middle and first AP 
- last_first_AP_width_ratio: average of ratio of ap_width between last and first AP 
- last_first_AP_peak_threshold_ratio: average of ratio of (v_peak-v_threshold) between last and first AP 
- last_first_AP_fast_trough_ratio: average of ratio of v_fast_trough between last and first AP 
- avg_injected_current_pA: average of injected current during stimulus period (pA)

GROUP 3:
Kink detection (per‑peak → per‑sweep averages)
kink_detection.py

High‑level flow (plain English):
1. Find the main upstroke
2. Find candidate dV/dt peaks between threshold and max upstroke, 
   - Use both find_peaks and finding all local maxima in window
   - Compute per candidate: height, ratio to max upstroke, prominence, post-dip ratio, interval to main upstroke (ms), width, and sign changes
   - Key Terms: 
     - Prominence: how much a peak sticks out from surroundings
	 - Post dip ratio: how much the signal drops after the kink before rising again ((kink height - lowest point after kink) / max_upstroke)
	 - Width: width at half-height of the kink in dV/dt
	 - Sign changes: How many times the signal wiggles up and down after the kink (indicative of noise)

3. Combine candidates from both methods (union)
4. Remove noisy / fluctuation-like peaks (anti-wiggle filter)
5. Stage 1: strict rule-based selection
- Filters for kink ratio, prominence, post-dip ratio, and interval
- Pick strongest candidate with the max height
6. Optional Stage 2 selection with relaxed thresholds 


Listed metrics (per‑sweep averages):
- pct_spikes_with_kink: average of % of spikes that have a pre-upstroke dV/dt peak (“kink”)
- avg_kink_interval_ms: average of (t_max_dvdt - t_kink_peak)
- avg_kink_ratio: average of (kink_dvdt_peak / max_dvdt)
- avg_kink_height_dVdt: average of kink_dvdt_peak (mV/ms)

GROUP 4:
Sav Gol Filtering (per-sweep and per‑cell averages)
sav_gol.py
Note: Sav gol filter is applied ONLY on the baseline period

Listed metrics (per-sweep averages):
- filtered_resting_vm_mean_mV: Average resting membrane potential after Savitzky-Golay filtering
- frequency_pre_filter: Spike frequency before filtering over the baseline period
- frequency_post_filter: Spike frequency after filtering over the baseline period

Listed metrics (per-cell averages:)
- drift_range: Average of total voltage range across baseline (max - min) after Savitzky-Golay filtering
- d_std: Average of standard deviation of baseline voltage after Savitzky-Golay filtering
- normalized_signal_variability: How much the signal changes over time, normalized by duration, after Savitzky-Golay filtering
  - Split each sweep into fixed windows and compute the mean voltage
  - Compute absolute difference between consecutive windows and sum
  - Normalize by time (baseline duration)
  - Average across all sweeps
- filtered_RMP_derivative: Average slope (dV/dt) of baseline voltage over time after Savitzky-Golay filtering
  - Split each sweep into fixed windows
  - Compute mean voltage for each window
  - Compute gradient of these mean values over time (window size)

Group 5:
Sag Current (per-sweep average)
sag_current.py
Note: Sag is only computed for negative current sweeps, which should have no spikes
Key variables:
- v_baseline: most negative point in first 80 seconds of stimulus period
- v_min: most negative point in stimulus period (peak hyperpolarization)
- v_steady: averaged voltage in last 80 seconds of stimulus period

Listed metrics (per-sweep averages for negative currents only):
- sag_voltage_mV: How much the neuron recovered from the deepest point (v_steady - v_min)
- sag_ratio: Fraction of how much of the extra drop (baseline -> min) is recovered by the end (v_steady - v_min) / (v_baseline - v_min)
- sag_percent: sag_ratio * 100
- peak_hyperpolarization_mV: How much additional drop happened after the initial response (v_baseline - v_min)

Group 6:
Other cell-level metrics
- input_resistance: input_resistance.py (line 22)
- grand_average_resting_vm: run_analysis.py (line 1248)
- filtered_grand_average_resting_vm: sav_gol_filter.py (line 573)
- current_threshold_pA: spike_detection_new.py (line 1568)

Listed metrics (defined cell-level):
- input_resistance: Apply Ohm's law to compute slope of I-V curve (over averaged voltage and averaged current) within stimulus period for first 8 sweeps with no spikes
  Quality metric: R² > 0.9 required for reliable result
- grand_average_resting_vm: Average of resting_vm_mean_mV across ALL sweeps in recording  
- filtered_grand_average_resting_vm: Average of filtered_resting_vm_mean_mV across ALL sweeps after Savitzky-Golay filtering
- current_threshold_pA: Minimum injected current needed to evoke at least one spike (excitability measure)
